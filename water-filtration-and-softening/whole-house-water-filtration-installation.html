Frequently Asked Questions

A whole-house water filtration system is designed to filter water as it enters your home, ensuring that all subsequent water used for drinking, cooking, bathing, and cleaning is purified at the point of entry.

Installing a whole-house water filtration system improves water quality throughout your home by removing contaminants, sediments, and chemicals, thus protecting your health and extending the life of your plumbing and appliances.

The installation of a whole-house water filtration system typically takes between 2 to 4 hours, depending on the complexity of the plumbing and the specific system being installed.

Most whole-house water filtration systems require regular maintenance, including changing filter cartridges every 3 to 12 months, depending on water usage and the system type. Some systems may also need periodic inspections to ensure optimal performance.

A properly installed and maintained whole-house water filtration system should have minimal impact on water pressure. If any reduction is noticed, it may be due to a clogged filter, which can be resolved by conducting regular maintenance.
