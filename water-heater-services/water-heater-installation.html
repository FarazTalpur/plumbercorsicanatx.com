Frequently Asked Questions

The size of the water heater depends on the number of people in your household and your water usage habits. Generally, a 40-50 gallon tank is sufficient for a family of four, but homes with high water usage may require a larger capacity.

Installation typically takes about 2-3 hours, assuming there are no complications. This includes removing the old unit, installing the new one, and testing the installation for leaks and proper operation.

Permit requirements vary by location, but many local building codes require a permit for water heater installation to ensure safety and compliance with regulations. Check with your local municipality to determine what's needed in your area.

A tank water heater stores and heats a large volume of water in a reservoir, while a tankless water heater heats water on demand, providing an endless supply of hot water and potentially saving energy by not maintaining a hot water reserve.

Regular maintenance can help extend the lifespan of your water heater. This includes flushing the tank annually to remove sediment buildup, inspecting the anode rod, setting the thermostat to 120°F, and checking for leaks or signs of corrosion.
